## Why Tier 3 Exists

Tier 1 (Foundation) makes and proves decisions. Tier 2 (Enterprise) hardens and scales them. Tier 3 is fundamentally different — it's not about running your organization better. It's about:

- **Surviving** institutional failure before it happens (COLLAPSE)
- **Modeling** how your decisions affect society (SGAS)
- **Dominating** your industry with deep vertical integration (VERTICALS)
- **Governing** at nation-scale (FRONTIER)

The customers who need Tier 3 aren't just companies. They're institutions whose decisions affect millions of people.

## Pillar 9: COLLAPSE — Institutional Failure Simulation

*"Simulate how your institution could fail — before it does"*

**Pricing:** $2,000,000 – $6,000,000 per year

COLLAPSE deploys 18 adversarial agents across 7 societal failure domains to simulate how your institution could collapse. This is NOT red-teaming (that's STRESS-TEST). Red-teaming attacks decisions and systems. COLLAPSE attacks your institution's legitimacy, trust, democratic processes, and societal role.

**Why Tier 3, not Tier 2:** STRESS-TEST (Tier 2) asks "will this decision fail?" COLLAPSE asks "will this decision erode democratic norms, harm minorities, or collapse institutional legitimacy?" Fundamentally different magnitude. Only mature, sophisticated customers are ready for existential institutional analysis.

**Why it's standalone (not part of STRESS-TEST):** Institutional failure ≠ technical red-teaming. The 18 agents map to societal failure domains, not technical vulnerabilities. A red team finds SQL injection. COLLAPSE finds "your AI-assisted policing decisions are eroding community trust in law enforcement."

### The 18 Adversarial Agents (7 Failure Domains)

| Domain | Agents | What They Attack |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| **Democratic Process** | Democratic Erosion Agent, Legitimacy Collapse Agent | Does this decision undermine democratic participation or institutional legitimacy? |
| **Social Fabric** | Cultural Erasure Agent, Minority Harm Agent, Freedom of Association Agent | Does this decision erase culture, harm minorities, or restrict association? |
| **Information Integrity** | Narrative Weaponization Agent, Free Speech Agent, Due Process Agent | Can this decision be weaponized as propaganda, restrict speech, or violate due process? |
| **Economic Stability** | Economic Instability Agent, Systemic Risk Agent | Could this decision destabilize markets or create cascading economic failures? |
| **Environmental** | Environmental Externality Agent | Hidden environmental damage from this decision? |
| **Temporal** | Temporal Decay Agent | How does this decision degrade over time? What looks safe now but fails in 10 years? |
| **Systemic** | Systemic Risk Agent, Cascading Failure Agent | Does this create interdependencies that could cascade across systems? |

### Unique Analytical Concepts

| Concept | What It Does |
|---------|-------------|
| **Trust Delta** | Measures the change in institutional trust caused by a decision — "this decision reduces public trust by 0.12 points" |
| **Failure Envelopes** | Maps the boundaries of safe operation — "you're safe within these parameters, but crossing any of these 4 thresholds triggers collapse" |
| **CollapseOrchestrator** | Coordinates all 18 agents into a unified institutional failure assessment |

## Pillar 10: SGAS — Societal-Scale Governance Simulation

*"Model governance at the scale of society"*

**Pricing:** $2,000,000 – $8,000,000 per year

SGAS (Synthetic Governance Agent System) simulates governance at societal scale — entire populations, departments, organizations, and ecosystems of actors. The Council deliberates at boardroom scale (15 agents). SGAS simulates at civilizational scale (thousands of agents across 5 classes).

**Why Tier 3, not Tier 2:** Different architecture, different scale. The Council has individual agents (CFO, CTO). SGAS simulates entire institutions, populations, and meta-governance systems.

**Why it's standalone (not part of THE COUNCIL):** The Council is boardroom-scale deliberation. SGAS is societal-scale simulation. A city planning a new transit system doesn't need 15 C-suite agents debating — it needs 10,000 citizen agents, 50 business agents, 12 government agency agents, and 5 environmental advocacy agents all interacting in a simulated ecosystem.

### The 5 Agent Classes

| Class | What It Simulates | Scale |

| **Decision Agents** | AI systems that make or recommend decisions | Multiple autonomous decision-makers interacting |

| **Adversarial Agents** | Dedicated attackers of governance systems | Strategic adversaries, not just red-teamers |
| **Observer Agents** | Independent monitors that watch and report | Journalism, watchdog, audit functions |
