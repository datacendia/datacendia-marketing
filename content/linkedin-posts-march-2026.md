**Suggested: 2-3 days after Post 1**

We gave certificate verification its own subdomain.

Here's why that's not a vanity move:

When Datacendia generates a Regulator's Receipt — a cryptographically signed record of how an AI decision was made — the recipient needs to verify it.

Previously: app.datacendia.com/verify/export/abc123
Now: verify.datacendia.com/export/abc123

Same backend. Same cryptography. Different trust signal.

A dedicated verification endpoint tells the examiner:
"This isn't a feature. This is infrastructure."

It's the difference between a bank printing your statement from their app vs. sending it on letterhead with a routing number.

Regulators don't verify claims inside the vendor's product.
They verify claims against the vendor's infrastructure.

Now they can.

**Suggested: 2-3 days after Post 5**

We open-sourced the governance standard. Then we forgot to link to it.

The DDGI Framework — Datacendia Decision Governance Infrastructure — has been public on GitHub since launch.

But until this week, the marketing site's DGI page discussed the framework without linking to the repository.

That's fixed.

datacendia.com/dgi.html now links directly to:
github.com/datacendia/decision-governance-infrastructure

The framework defines 5 governance primitives:
1. Attestation Anchors — discovery-time proof
2. Deliberation Capture — adversarial consensus recording
3. Evidentiary Continuity — chain-of-custody for decisions

DDGI is vendor-neutral. CC BY 4.0 licensed.

You don't need Datacendia to implement DDGI.
