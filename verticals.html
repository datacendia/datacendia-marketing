Sports / Football — Crisis Immunization for the Transfer Window

Transfer governance, PSR/FFP compliance monitoring, wage-to-revenue drift detection, and institutional memory that survives manager turnover. Every transfer decision deliberated, documented, and defensible.

The 9 Primitives in Football

  • P1 Discovery-Time Proof: When was the FFP exposure identified? Timestamped, sealed, defensible to the Premier League commission
  • P3 Override Accountability: When the chairman overrules the sporting director on a transfer, the override is logged and tracked to outcome
  • P4 Continuity Memory: Scouting intelligence and tactical reasoning survives manager turnover — "we tried this type of player in 2021"
  • P5 Drift Detection: Flags wage-to-revenue ratio creep across windows before PSR thresholds are breached

Compliance Frameworks

  • Premier League PSR: Profit & Sustainability Rules monitoring with window-over-window drift alerts
  • UEFA CFSR: Club Financial Sustainability Regulations — squad cost ratio (70%), football earnings deviation
  • EFL FFP: Championship/League One financial compliance with salary cap monitoring
  • FIFA Agent Regulations: Intermediary fee tracking, dual representation flags, related-party transaction detection

Decision Types

  • Transfer Decisions: Multi-agent deliberation with FFP impact, sporting risk, financial analysis, and board governance review
  • Wage Structures: Base vs. performance-linked analysis with concentration risk warnings
  • Manager Succession: Continuity Memory preserves tactical philosophy and scouting intelligence
  • Squad Planning: Window-level budget allocation with PSR headroom projection

War Game: Everton PSR Breach

Everton received a 10-point deduction for breaching PSR limits. Drift Detection (P5) would have flagged the wage-to-revenue ratio creep across 3 transfer windows. Discovery-Time Proof (P1) would have established exactly when the board was made aware of the trajectory. The breach didn't happen overnight — it was a governance failure over years.

Healthcare — Decision Governance for Patient Safety

Datacendia governs the decision process, not the clinical recommendation. It is not a diagnostic tool and does not fall under FDA Clinical Decision Support guidance (21st Century Cures Act §3060). It captures deliberation, documents protocol deviations, and produces evidence for Joint Commission reviews.

Why Health Systems Need This

  • Sentinel Event Prevention: P1 (Discovery-Time Proof) establishes when the care team identified a risk — the core question in every root cause analysis
  • Clinical Protocol Documentation: P3 (Override Accountability) logs every deviation from established clinical protocols with justification
  • Physician Rotation Continuity: P4 (Continuity Memory) preserves institutional knowledge through resident rotations, attending changes, and department restructuring
  • Compliance Erosion Monitoring: P5 (Drift Detection) flags when protocol adherence rates decline before they trigger adverse events

EHR & System Integration

  • Data Ingestion: Designed to ingest structured data from major EHR systems (Epic, Cerner/Oracle Health, MEDITECH) via HL7 FHIR, CSV, or database connectors
  • On-Premises Only: PHI never leaves your network. Zero-copy architecture queries data in place — no ETL, no cloud exposure
  • Evidence Export: Decision packets export as PDF/JSON for Joint Commission, CMS, or state survey readiness
  • No CDS Classification: Datacendia is a governance tool, not a clinical decision support system. It does not recommend diagnoses or treatments

Clinical Decision Types

  • Resource Allocation: Bed management, staffing ratios, equipment procurement with compliance evidence
  • Policy Governance: Clinical pathway adoption, formulary decisions, vendor selection
  • Risk Committee: Patient safety committee deliberation with sealed dissent capture
  • Regulatory Response: CMS CoP compliance documentation, HIPAA breach response coordination

Compliance Frameworks

  • HIPAA / HITECH: PHI stays on your infrastructure. BAA available for private cloud
  • Joint Commission: Sentinel event root cause analysis evidence generation
  • CMS CoPs: Conditions of Participation documentation and audit trail
  • CMS AI Transparency: Designed to support emerging CMS requirements for AI transparency in healthcare decision-making

Financial Services — Exam-Ready Decision Governance

When the OCC examiner asks "prove your risk committee considered this exposure" — hand them a cryptographically sealed evidence packet with the full deliberation, every dissent, and the human sign-off. That's what Datacendia produces.

Model Risk Management (SR 11-7)

  • Override Documentation: P3 (Override Accountability) maps directly to SR 11-7 / OCC 2011-12 model override documentation requirements
  • Model Validation Evidence: When humans override a model recommendation, the override is logged with justification and tracked to outcome
  • Examiner-Ready Exports: Evidence packets formatted for OCC, Fed, FDIC, and state banking exam review
  • Risk Appetite Monitoring: P5 (Drift Detection) flags when risk-taking deviates from board-approved risk appetite statements

Regulatory Frameworks

  • SR 11-7 / OCC 2011-12: Model risk management with override tracking and validation evidence
  • Basel III/IV: Capital adequacy decision documentation with risk-weighted asset impact analysis
  • SOX §302/§404: Internal control evidence for financial reporting decisions
  • BSA/AML: Suspicious activity decision trails with sealed deliberation for FinCEN reporting
  • DORA: Digital Operational Resilience Act ICT risk management documentation

Decision Types

  • Credit Exposure Approvals: Multi-agent risk assessment with concentration limits
  • Risk Committee Decisions: Full deliberation capture with formal dissent filing
  • Exception Handling: Policy exception documentation with override accountability
  • Vendor & Counterparty Risk: Third-party risk assessment with evidence packets

War Game: Silicon Valley Bank

SVB's $209B collapse was a governance failure: duration risk was identifiable, liquidity concentration was flaggable, and interest rate sensitivity was modelable — all from data the bank already had. Drift Detection (P5) would have flagged the deviation from risk appetite. Discovery-Time Proof (P1) would have timestamped when management was made aware.
